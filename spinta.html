Anomaly Detection in Transactional Data

June, 2018 - August, 2018

Banking operations include many daily, periodic, and aperiodic activities and transactions performed by or affecting numerous stakeholders such as employees, customers, debtors, and external entities. The complex nature of these activities and transactions necessitate constant monitoring to ensure that neither the bank nor its stakeholders are adversely affected by various events that may be malicious, random, or occurring due to inevitable business cycles. The goal during the internship period was to build Machine Learning models for identification of outliers in daily user activity data so that any unusual activity can be predicted and/or detected in order to make online banking platforms a safer place.

Knowledge Gained

  1. Handling extremely skewed data with the majority class representing 99.34% of the total data.
  2. Preprocessing involved in time series data
  3. Implementing Down and Upsampling of data for handling skewness.
  4. Using varied metrics, losses and optimisation techniques since metrics like accuracy are not a correct measure when handling skewed data.
  5. Increasing sensitivity of the ML models rather than increasing their ability to classify data.
  6. Different clustering methods for both visualisation and drawing inferences about the data
  7. Implementing parallelism in models for handling data from many input streams.
