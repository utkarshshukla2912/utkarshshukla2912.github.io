Developing Models For Demographic Based Population And Economical Estimation

July, 2018 - Present

The Goal is to estimate Economical attributes of different areas in a country. This process involves the population estimation of an area based on Digital Elevation Models after the population is calculated attempts are made to devise models to estimate the economical attributes such as lifestyle and revenue generated by the people in the area. This would provide a detailed analysis of the different areas under consideration and help in the estimation of the feasibility of a policy that the government attempts to deploy in the area.

  1. Digital Elevation Models which contain information about elevation at different latitudes and longitudes is collected from NASA's open repository.
  2. Areas are classified into different landforms such as low plains, hills, mountains, human settlement
    3. The areas where we identify human settlement we try to determine the kind of human structures eg Residential, Commercial. This is done by finding out the height of the man-made structure
  3. These features combined with Satellite Imagery would then be used to analyse the demography

Knowledge Gained

  1. Understanding of Digital Elevation Models provided by NASA.
  2. Importance of Terrain Elevation in landmass classification.
  3. Object detection, recognition, and localization from Satellite Imagery.
  4. Factors and features that define the Economic System of a human settlement.
